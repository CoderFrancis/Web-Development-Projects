Milky Way Galaxy

About the Milky Way

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our Solar System, with the name describing the galaxy's appearance from Earth: a hazy band of light seen in the night sky formed from stars that cannot be individually distinguished by the naked eye.

The Milky Way is a barred spiral galaxy with an estimated visible diameter of 100,000–200,000 light-years. It is estimated to contain 100–400 billion stars and at least that number of planets.

The Solar System is located within the disk, about 27,000 light-years from the Galactic Center, on the inner edge of the Orion Arm, one of the spiral-shaped concentrations of gas and dust. The Milky Way is surrounded by several smaller satellite galaxies.
